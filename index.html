Goal-line technology

It is now possible to quickly and precisely determine if the entire ball has crossed the goal line with the use of goal-line technology. It makes it simple for referees to tell whether the opposing team scored. Given that the information is given in a split second or less, the referee will react immediately. It is impossible for gaming to be stopped or interrupted because to this layout. The only individuals who are able to view the signal on their timepieces are the match officials.

Photo finish technology

In track and field, a photo finish is used to precisely determine the finishing positions of racing events. Modern track and field competitions use digital cameras instead of the older film technology. Digital cameras have the advantage of taking sequential 1-pixel-wide images of the finish line using a 1-dimensional array sensor. Frame rates can reach 10,000 frames per second because only one line of the CCD is read out at once. The 2008 Summer Olympics saw the debut of some of the fastest timekeeping technology yet, with cameras that take pictures 3,000 times per second.
